Corpus Hermeticum

Book IX: Universal Mind (On Thought and Sensation)

Universal Mind explores the difference between divine knowledge (through mind) and human knowledge (through senses). Only mind can know God. The senses know only shadows.

Translation: Brian P. Copenhaver (1992)

Two Kinds of Knowledge

1. "There are two kinds of knowledge: one through the senses, one through the mind. The knowledge of the senses is human. The knowledge of the mind is divine."
2. "The senses show you becoming — things that change, things that pass away. The mind shows you being — things that are eternal, things that are real."
3. "Do not trust the senses. They lie. They show you a world of shadows. They show you separation where there is unity."
4. "Trust the mind. It sees truth. It sees unity. It sees God."
Epistemology: This is Hermetic theory of knowledge. Senses = doxa (opinion). Mind = episteme (true knowledge). Only mind can know the divine.

What Mind Sees

5. "What does mind see? It sees the One. It sees that all things are one. It sees that separation is illusion."
6. "The senses see many things: colors, sounds, textures. The mind sees one thing: being itself."
7. "The senses say: 'I am here, you are there.' The mind says: 'We are one.'"
8. "The senses say: 'This is good, that is bad.' The mind says: 'All is God.'"
Non-Duality: This is radical non-duality. Not just "we are all connected" — "we are all one." The Hermeticist sees through the illusion of separation.

Training the Mind

9. "How do you train the mind? First, withdraw from the senses. Close the eyes. Stop the ears. Turn inward."
10. "Second, empty the thoughts. Let them pass like clouds. Do not grasp them. Do not follow them."
11. "Third, wait in silence. The mind will speak. It will show you what the senses cannot see."
12. "This is meditation. This is the Hermetic path. This is how you become God."
Meditation Practice: Withdrawal (pratyahara) → Emptying → Silence → Revelation. This is identical to yogic practice. East and West meet here.

Why Most Fail

13. "Why do most humans fail to know God? Because they trust the senses. They believe what they see. They believe what they touch."
14. "They say: 'Show me God. Let me see Him. Let me touch Him.' But God cannot be seen. God cannot be touched. God can only be known by mind."
15. "They are like a man who tries to see with his ears. They use the wrong instrument."
The Error: Trying to know the divine through senses is like trying to hear colors. Wrong tool. Mind is the only instrument that can know God.

The Promise

16. "If you train the mind, if you withdraw from the senses, if you wait in silence — you will know God."
17. "Not believe in God. Not have faith in God. Know God. Directly. Immediately. Certainly."
18. "This is gnosis. This is the Hermetic promise. This is the path."
Gnosis vs. Faith: Christianity says "believe." Hermeticism says "know." Faith is secondhand. Gnosis is firsthand. The Hermeticist does not believe — they know.

Practice: Daily meditation: 10 minutes withdrawal from senses. Sit in silence. Let thoughts pass. Wait for mind to speak.

"Trust the mind. It sees truth."
