Corpus Hermeticum

Book I: Poimandres (The Shepherd of Men)

Poimandres is the first and most important book of the Corpus Hermeticum. It describes Hermes' vision of the Divine Mind (Nous) and the creation of the cosmos. This is the foundation of all Hermetic philosophy.

Translation: Brian P. Copenhaver (1992) — Scholar's standard edition

The Vision Begins

1. Once upon a time, my thinking of the beings was activated, and my thoughts soared to the heights, whereas all my senses were restrained as are those of people who are heavy with sleep from satiety or toil or the body's growth.
2. It seemed to me that a boundless being called me by name and said: "What do you want to hear and see, what do you want to learn and know?"
3. "Who are you?" I said.
4. "I am Poimandres," he said, "Mind of Sovereignty. I know what you want, and I am with you everywhere."
5. "I want to learn about the beings and to understand their nature and to know God," I said.
6. "So be it," he said.

The Vision of Light

7. Saying this, he changed aspect, and immediately all things were revealed to me in an instant, and I saw an indescribable sight. All things had become a pleasant light that charmed my sight.
8. Then a frightful and dreadful darkness descended sinuously, appearing to me as if it had changed into some sort of damp and turbulent nature, belching out smoke as from a fire and a sort of lamentable cry that issued from it.
9. Then out of the heart of the light a holy word mounted on the moist nature, and unalloyed fire leapt up from the moist nature to the height above. The fire was nimble and penetrating and active as well. And the air, being light, followed the fire and rose up to the fire from earth and water.
10. The fire descended from the height to the moist nature. The earth remained with it, having been separated from the water. The moist nature was agitated by the word, and it emitted a soundless shout.
Symbolism: Light = Divine Mind. Darkness = unformed matter. The Word (Logos) separates and orders the elements. This is Hermetic cosmogony — creation through divine speech.

The Creation of Man

12. Then Poimandres said to me: "Did you understand what you saw?"
13. "I shall know," I said.
14. "I am the light you saw with your mind," he said, "I am Mind, your God, who is prior to the moist nature that appeared out of the darkness. The luminous word that issues from Mind is the son of God."
15. "What does this mean?" I asked.
16. "Learn this: what in you sees and hears is the word of the Lord; Mind is the Father God. They are not separate from each other, for their union is life."
17. "Thank you," I said.
18. "Understand the light," he said, "and make yourself at home with it."
19. Speaking thus, he gazed at me for a long time, so that I trembled at his look. But when he raised his head, I saw in my Mind the light — now with myriad powers — and a world that had come to be without limit.

The Descent of Man

21. When Poimandres had said these things, he gazed at me steadily. I was amazed at his look. He continued: "Did you notice that the one who knows himself goes to God?"
22. "I have not yet heard what you promise," I said.
23. "So be it," he said. "The Father of all consists of light and life, from which Man was born."
24. "Mind, the Father, being light and life, brought forth Man similar to himself. Man, being beautiful, like the image of his Father — for God really loved his own image — gave over to him all his own works."
25. "After Man saw the creation of the Father in the fire, he wanted to create as well. The Father consented. He transferred the form to Man, and Man, having received the Father's entire work, tried to create."
26. "Man stepped out of the contemplation of the Father, having entered into the sphere of creation, and he loved his own body."
The Fall: Man descends through the spheres, taking on the nature of each. This is not sin — it is necessary for creation. But Man forgets his divine origin.

The Ascent Back to God

26. "Therefore, those who have no knowledge of God are the majority of humankind. They are enslaved to the body and to the senses. But those who know themselves — they enter into the Father."
27. "This is the way of the wise: to know God, to know oneself, to return to the source."
28. "The body is the temple of God. The senses are the guardians. But the Mind — the Mind is God himself within you."
29. "When the body dies, the senses return to their sources. The passions dissolve. And the human being ascends through the spheres, leaving behind what each sphere claims."
30. "Then, naked of all accretions, the soul comes to the eighth sphere — the realm of the fixed stars. There, it sings hymns to the Father."
31. "And the powers sing through the human being. They rush into God. And the human being enters into God. This is the good, the end for those who have received knowledge: to become God."
The Return: This is the Hermetic path — not salvation by grace, but ascent by knowledge (gnosis). Know yourself, know God, become God.

The Final Teaching

32. "This is the mystery that has been hidden until this day: God loved Man more than any other being. Man is a great wonder, a living being worthy of reverence and honor."
33. "For Man goes even further than the gods, or at least is their equal. The gods are immortal, but Man is mortal. Yet through knowledge, Man becomes immortal."
34. "Let the one who has understanding understand. God has given you everything. Use it. Become what you are."
