Corpus Hermeticum

Book XIII: Secret Discourse on Rebirth (Palingenesis)

The Secret Discourse is the most esoteric text in the Corpus Hermeticum. It describes the actual process of spiritual rebirth — how a human becomes God. This was taught orally, in secret, to prepared disciples. Tat asks, Hermes teaches.

Translation: Brian P. Copenhaver (1992)

Tat's Request

1. Tat said to Hermes: "Father, you promised me the discourse on rebirth. You said you would reveal it to me when I was ready. I am ready. Teach me."
2. Hermes said: "I am silent, son. I am seeking the divine within me. I am preparing to speak the unspeakable."
3. "Speak, Father. I am listening with the ears of the mind. I am ready to receive."
4. "This discourse cannot be written. It cannot be spoken. It can only be shown. But I will try."
Secret Teaching: This text was reserved for advanced disciples. Not because it's dangerous — because it's useless without preparation. Tat has completed the earlier stages. Now he's ready.

The Ten Powers

5. "Rebirth requires ten powers. The first is knowledge of God. The second is joy. The third is self-control."
6. "The fourth is dispassion. The fifth is truth. The sixth is goodness."
7. "The seventh is life. The eighth is light. The ninth is word (logos). The tenth is mind (nous)."
8. "These ten powers grow from one seed: faith. They mature through practice. They flower into rebirth."
Decad: Ten is the number of completion (Pythagorean decad). These powers are not intellectual — they are lived. Each must be embodied before the next can grow.

The Death of the Senses

9. "Before rebirth, there must be death. Not physical death — death of the senses. The senses must be silenced."
10. "The body must become still. The passions must be stilled. The thoughts must be stilled."
11. "In that stillness, something new is born. Not you — the God within you."
12. "This is the mystery: you die to yourself so that God can live through you."
Mystical Death: This is the core of all mysticism. Die before you die. The ego-self must surrender so the divine-self can emerge. Christianity calls it "dying to self." Hermeticism calls it "rebirth."

The Birth of the Divine

13. "When the senses are silent, the mind awakens. When the mind awakens, God speaks."
14. "You will feel a warmth. A light. A presence. This is God being born in you."
15. "You will not be the same. You cannot be the same. You are reborn."
16. "From this moment, you are no longer human. You are God in human form."
The Transformation: This is not metaphor. This is ontology. You literally become divine. Not "like God" — God. This is the Hermetic promise.

The Hymn of Rebirth

The Prayer of Thanksgiving

"Every power of heaven sings through me. I sing through every power of heaven.

"Knowledge, joy, self-control, dispassion, truth, goodness, life, light, word, mind — all ten powers sing within me.

"God is all. God is one. God is me. I am God.

"This is the rebirth. This is the mystery. This is the end and the beginning."

The Hymn: This prayer concludes Book XIII. It's meant to be recited after the rebirth experience — as thanksgiving and as affirmation.

Living as God

17. "What happens after rebirth? You live differently. You see differently. You act differently."
18. "You no longer serve the body. You no longer serve the senses. You no longer serve the passions."
19. "You serve God. You are God. There is no difference."
20. "This is the end of the path. This is the beginning of the work."
After Rebirth: The work doesn't end — it begins. Now you live as God in the world. This is the hardest part. Rebirth is instantaneous. Living as God takes a lifetime.

Practice: This text is meant to be read after completing Books I, IV, and IX. Do not rush. Rebirth cannot be forced — it happens when you're ready.
