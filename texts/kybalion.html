The Kybalion

The Seven Hermetic Principles — Three Initiates (1908)

Note: The Kybalion is modern (1908), not ancient. But the Seven Principles are solid Hermetic philosophy. Read this alongside the actual Corpus Hermeticum for the full picture.

I The Principle of Mentalism

"The All is Mind; the Universe is Mental."

Everything that exists is ultimately mental in nature. The universe is a thought in the mind of the All. Your reality is shaped by consciousness — your own and the universal.

Implication: If the universe is mental, then changing your mind changes your reality. This is not "positive thinking" — this is ontology.

Practice

Before any work, affirm: "This is mental. I am working within mind." Feel the shift from material to mental framing. Meditate on: "What if everything I perceive is thought?"

II The Principle of Correspondence

"As above, so below; as below, so above."

The macrocosm reflects the microcosm. The universe and the individual are mirrors. Study one to understand the other. The atom and the solar system share the same structure.

Implication: Your inner state creates your outer reality. Fix the inner, the outer follows. Also: study astronomy to understand psychology, study biology to understand cosmology.

Practice

When facing an outer problem, ask: "What inner state does this correspond to?" When working on inner growth, ask: "How will this manifest externally?" Track the correlations.

III The Principle of Vibration

"Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates."

Everything is energy in motion. Matter is slow vibration. Light is fast vibration. Thought is faster. The difference between you and a stone is vibrational rate.

Implication: You can change your vibration deliberately. This is the core skill of Hermeticism. High vibration = clarity, energy, manifestation. Low vibration = confusion, fatigue, resistance.

Practice

Multiple times daily, check: "What is my vibration right now?" (1-10 scale). Learn the feel of each level. Practice shifting it: breath work, movement, music, intention. Track what raises/lowers you.

IV The Principle of Polarity

"Everything is dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites."

Opposites are the same thing at different points on a spectrum. Hot and cold are both temperature. Love and hate are both intense emotion. You can slide along the pole.

Implication: Fear can become courage (same energy, different pole). Depression can become focus (same withdrawal, different direction). Don't fight the energy — redirect it.

Practice

When experiencing a negative pole (fear, anger, sadness), don't suppress it. Acknowledge the energy, then consciously slide toward the positive pole. Fear → Courage. Anger → Boundaries. Sadness → Depth.

V The Principle of Rhythm

"Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall."

Reality is cyclical, not linear. Days, seasons, breath, heartbeat, markets, relationships, moods — all rhythm. The pendulum swings both ways.

Implication: When you're high, prepare for low. When you're low, know high is coming. Don't fight the rhythm — learn to surf it. Neutralise the swing through awareness.

Practice

Track your rhythms: energy, mood, creativity, social capacity. Find your cycles (daily, weekly, lunar, seasonal). Plan accordingly: push during high, rest during low. Don't fight the tide.

VI The Principle of Cause and Effect

"Every cause has its effect; every effect has its cause."

Nothing happens by chance. Everything is the result of prior causes. Most people are effects, not causes — pushed around by external forces. The Hermeticist becomes a cause.

Implication: Stop reacting. Start initiating. Every action you take is a cause that will have effects. Choose your causes deliberately.

Practice

Before any significant action, ask: "What effects will this cause?" After any significant event, ask: "What causes led to this?" Take radical responsibility. No victims, only causes and effects.

VII The Principle of Gender

"Gender is in everything; everything has its masculine and feminine principles."

Not about biological sex. About energetic principles. Masculine = active, projecting, giving. Feminine = receptive, nurturing, creating. Both exist in everything. Creation requires both.

Implication: Balance both within yourself. Too masculine = burnout, aggression. Too feminine = passivity, overwhelm. Integrated = whole.

Practice

Audit your life: Where are you over-using masculine (doing, pushing, forcing)? Where are you over-using feminine (receiving, waiting, allowing)? Consciously practice the under-used pole.
