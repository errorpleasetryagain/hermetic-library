Corpus Hermeticum

Book IV: The Cup (The Krater)

The Cup is the vessel of divine knowledge (gnosis). Those who immerse themselves in it receive mind and become complete. Those who refuse remain incomplete. This is the Hermetic baptism — not water, but knowledge.

Translation: Brian P. Copenhaver (1992)

The Cup Descends

1. "What shall I say about the soul, or what great thing shall I proclaim? For if I speak of the soul, I must speak of God."
2. "Listen: God sent the Cup down to earth. He commanded a herald to proclaim to the hearts of men: 'Immerse yourself in this Cup, you who are able. You who believe that you will ascend to Him who sent this Cup down. You who know for what purpose you were born.'"
3. "Some received the Cup and were baptized in the knowledge. They became complete humans. They received mind. They saw all things. They understood."
4. "But others mocked the Cup. They refused to immerse themselves. They remained incomplete. They received only partial knowledge. They walked in darkness."
The Cup = Gnosis: This is not physical baptism. The Cup is divine knowledge. Immersion is complete understanding. Those who drink become "complete humans" — teleios anthropos. This is the Hermetic goal.

Why Some Refuse

5. "Why do some refuse the Cup? Because they are drunk with ignorance. They are intoxicated by the darkness. They think they see, but they are blind."
6. "The Cup is offered to all, but not all can receive it. Some are not yet ready. Some are too attached to the body. Some are too attached to the senses."
7. "But those who are ready — they recognize the Cup. They run to it. They immerse themselves. They emerge complete."
Readiness: Not everyone is ready for gnosis at the same time. This is not condemnation — it is timing. Continue the work. The Cup will be offered again.

The Complete Human

8. "What is the complete human? The one who has received mind. The one who knows God. The one who knows himself."
9. "The incomplete human has body and soul, but not mind. He is like an animal. He follows the senses. He is driven by passion."
10. "The complete human has body, soul, and mind. He transcends the senses. He commands the passions. He sees with the eyes of God."
11. "This is the miracle: a human can become God. Not by grace, but by knowledge. Not by faith, but by understanding."
Tripartite Human: Body (soma) + Soul (psyche) + Mind (nous) = Complete. Most have only body and soul. The Hermeticist adds mind through gnosis.

The Path to the Cup

12. "How do you reach the Cup? First, hate the body. Not destroy it — hate its domination. The body is the vessel of the soul, but it is also the prison."
13. "Second, hate the senses. Not blind yourself — hate their tyranny. The senses show you shadows, not reality."
14. "Third, hate the passions. Not become emotionless — hate their control. Passions are tools, not masters."
15. "When you have hated these sufficiently, the Cup will appear. You will recognize it. You will run to it. You will be complete."
Hate = Detachment: This is not self-destruction. It is detachment. The body is a tool — use it, don't be used by it. The senses are instruments — play them, don't let them play you.

The Reward

16. "What do you gain? Everything. You gain God. You gain yourself. You gain the universe."
17. "You will see all things. You will understand all things. You will be all things."
18. "This is the promise. This is the Cup. This is the path."
The Promise: Not salvation in heaven — deification on earth. Become God while alive. This is the Hermetic difference from Christianity, from Judaism, from Islam.

Practice: Meditate on: "What prevents me from receiving the Cup?" Write honestly. Then: "What would I need to release to be ready?"
