London Lodges

UGLE and esoteric lodges in Greater London

⚠️ Important: Lodge information changes. Always verify meeting times, locations, and visitor policies directly before attending. This list is a starting point for your research.

Quatuor Coronati Lodge No. 2076

Research Lodge

Location: Freemasons' Hall, Great Queen Street, London WC2B 5AZ

Focus: Masonic research, scholarship, lectures

Why it matters: The premier research lodge worldwide. Papers published in AQC (Ars Quatuor Coronatorum). Best for intellectual/esoteric study.

Contact: Via UGLE

Website: quatuorcoronati.com

Old King Solomon Lodge No. 2411

Traditional

Location: Various London temples

Focus: Traditional ritual work, philosophical study

Why it matters: Known for high-quality ritual and serious members. Good balance of esoteric and social.

Contact: Via UGLE lodge search

West London Lodge No. 2611

General

Location: West London

Focus: Mixed — ritual, charity, social

Why it matters: Well-established, good reputation. Worth visiting to compare.

Contact: Via UGLE lodge search

Scottish Rite (Rose Croix) Chapter

High Degree

Location: Various (requires Master Mason status first)

Focus: Esoteric, Kabbalistic, Hermetic teachings

Why it matters: This is where the deeper work happens. But you must be a Master Mason (3+ years typically) before joining.

Contact: Via UGLE

How to Research Lodges

  • UGLE Lodge Search: ugle.org.uk/membership/find-a-lodge
  • Visit multiple lodges: Don't join the first one. Attend 3-5 open evenings.
  • Ask about the culture: Some are social clubs, some are philosophical, some are esoteric. Find your fit.
  • Ask about age demographics: Some lodges are predominantly older members, some younger.
  • Ask about meeting frequency: Most meet 4-6 times per year. Some more, some less.
  • Location matters: You'll be attending for decades. Choose somewhere sustainable.

Questions to Ask at Open Evenings

  • "What's the typical age range of members?"
  • "How often does the lodge meet?"
  • "What proportion of meetings are ritual vs. business vs. social?"
  • "Are there study groups or philosophical discussions?"
  • "What's the joining process and timeline?"
  • "What are the annual dues?"
  • "Can I speak to some recent initiates about their experience?"
On Irregular Masonry: You'll encounter Co-Masonry (mixed gender), GLiF (Grande Loge Internationale Féminine), and other "irregular" bodies not recognised by UGLE. They often emphasise esoteric work more strongly. This is a personal choice — UGLE is mainstream and recognised worldwide; irregular bodies offer different experiences. Research both.
